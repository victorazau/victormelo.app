If you're a coach trying to decide between GoHighLevel and Kajabi, the short answer is this: Kajabi is a better fit if you're primarily selling courses and memberships with minimal client management needs; GoHighLevel is the stronger choice if you're running a coaching business that involves lead nurturing, sales pipelines, appointment booking, and automation at scale. I've implemented both platforms for coaching clients and the right answer really depends on what your business model looks like today — and where you want it to go.

## Key Takeaways

- **Kajabi excels** at course delivery, community features, and polished student experiences out of the box.
- **GoHighLevel excels** at CRM, sales funnels, multi-channel automation, and running multiple client sub-accounts.
- Kajabi starts at $149/mo and tops out at $399/mo; GoHighLevel starts at $97/mo with significantly more features per dollar.
- Coaches who want to eventually resell software or manage multiple client accounts should seriously consider GoHighLevel's SaaS model.
- Neither platform is objectively "better" — the winner depends on whether your revenue comes primarily from courses or from client relationships.

## What Is Kajabi and Who Is It For?

Kajabi is an all-in-one platform built specifically for knowledge creators — course instructors, membership site owners, podcast hosts, and coaches who deliver structured content to students. Launched in 2010, it has earned a strong reputation for its polished UI, reliable video hosting, and community features.

Kajabi's sweet spot is a solo coach or creator who wants to:
- Record a course, upload it, and sell it quickly
- Build a branded membership community
- Send broadcast emails to a subscriber list
- Accept payments without heavy technical setup

In my experience, Kajabi is genuinely one of the cleanest experiences for getting a course live. If your entire business model is "I create content, students buy it, they consume it," Kajabi handles that journey beautifully. The platform is also well-loved by coaches who prioritize aesthetics — the default templates look professional without much customization.

Where Kajabi starts to struggle is when your coaching business becomes more relationship-driven. If you're doing discovery calls, running group programs with active client management, following up with prospects across SMS and email, or building any kind of sales team workflow — Kajabi's CRM and automation capabilities feel limited very quickly.

## What Is GoHighLevel and Who Is It For?

GoHighLevel is a white-label marketing and CRM platform built primarily for agencies and service-based businesses — including coaching businesses that operate more like a client service than a content library. It covers funnels, websites, CRM pipelines, email marketing, SMS, appointment scheduling, reputation management, and now AI-powered features.

For coaches, GoHighLevel is the better fit when:
- You manage ongoing client relationships, not just course enrollment
- You want automated follow-up sequences across email, SMS, and voicemail drops
- You're running ads and need a proper lead pipeline
- You're considering hiring a VA or sales closer to manage your pipeline
- You want to eventually offer the software to your own clients as a white-labeled SaaS

I've set up GoHighLevel accounts for health coaches, business coaches, and life coaches — and in every case where they had an active sales process (strategy calls, discovery sessions, proposal follow-ups), GoHighLevel delivered far more value than Kajabi ever could. The platform's pipeline view alone saves coaches hours of manual tracking per week.

It's also worth noting that GoHighLevel has added a solid course and membership module. It's not as polished as Kajabi's, but for most coaching programs I've deployed it for, it gets the job done without requiring a separate subscription.

## Which Platform Wins for Specific Coaching Business Models?

Not all coaching businesses are the same. Here's how I'd break down the decision by model:

**Go with Kajabi if:**
- Your primary product is a self-paced course or membership with minimal 1:1 interaction
- You're a content creator first, coach second
- You want everything set up in a weekend with minimal learning curve
- Your audience is under 10,000 and you don't run paid ads aggressively
- You value community features deeply (Kajabi Communities is genuinely excellent)

**Go with GoHighLevel if:**
- You offer 1:1 or group coaching with an active sales process
- You're running discovery calls and need a pipeline to track prospects
- You want to automate follow-up via SMS, email, and voicemail
- You run paid ads and need a proper funnel + lead capture system
- You're planning to eventually manage multiple clients or resell the platform
- You want everything under one roof without stitching together 5 tools

For coaches who are serious about growth — running ads, booking strategy calls, managing group cohorts with active onboarding — GoHighLevel isn't just competitive with Kajabi, it's in a different category. ## Can You Use Both GoHighLevel and Kajabi Together?

Short answer: yes, and some advanced coaching businesses do exactly this. I've seen setups where GoHighLevel handles all the front-end marketing — ads, landing pages, CRM, follow-up automation, appointment booking — and Kajabi handles the back-end course delivery and community experience.

This hybrid approach makes sense only if:
- Your course content and community experience are central enough to your brand to justify Kajabi's premium
- You're generating enough revenue that an additional $149-$199/mo doesn't materially affect margins
- You have the technical confidence to connect the two via Zapier or webhooks

For most coaches I work with, this level of complexity isn't necessary. GoHighLevel's course module handles content delivery well enough that the majority of coaching programs don't need Kajabi's polish. But if you've built a deeply engaged community on Kajabi and are only adding GoHighLevel to improve your sales process, a hybrid setup is a legitimate option.
