GoHighLevel and Keap (formerly Infusionsoft) are two very different tools solving a similar problem: managing leads, automating follow-up, and closing more sales. After implementing both platforms for clients across different industries, my honest verdict is that GoHighLevel wins for agencies and growth-focused businesses, while Keap holds its ground for established small businesses that need a simple, stable CRM without the learning curve.

## Key Takeaways

- GoHighLevel starts at $97/mo and includes unlimited contacts, funnels, websites, and a built-in phone system — Keap starts at $299/mo for just 1,500 contacts.
- Keap is simpler to learn and has been battle-tested since 2001; GoHighLevel is more powerful but requires more setup time.
- GoHighLevel's SaaS/white-label mode is a unique revenue opportunity that Keap simply doesn't offer.
- For agencies managing multiple clients, GoHighLevel is the clear winner on both features and economics.
- Neither tool is perfect — your choice should depend on your team's technical comfort, your client volume, and how important reselling is to your business model.

## What Are GoHighLevel and Keap, Exactly?

GoHighLevel is an all-in-one marketing and sales platform built primarily for digital marketing agencies and the clients they serve. Launched in 2018, it combines a CRM, email marketing, SMS automation, funnel builder, website builder, calendar scheduling, reputation management, and a built-in phone/VoIP system under one roof. The platform also lets agencies white-label it and resell it as their own SaaS product.

Keap — which rebranded from Infusionsoft in 2019 — is a veteran CRM and marketing automation tool targeting small business owners who want to automate follow-up without needing a developer or a marketing agency. It's been around since 2001 and has a loyal user base, particularly among service-based businesses like contractors, consultants, and coaches.

I've implemented Keap for a handful of service businesses and GoHighLevel for dozens of agency clients, so this comparison comes from real-world use, not just feature sheet comparisons.

For solo operators or tiny teams, Keap's pricing might be justified if the simplicity saves you hours of setup. But for anyone running an agency or scaling past a few hundred contacts, GoHighLevel delivers dramatically more value per dollar.

## Which Platform Has Better Automation?

Automation is the core of both tools, and this is where the comparison gets nuanced.

Keap's automation builder (called "Campaigns") has been around for over a decade. It's mature, reliable, and visual. You build sequences by dragging elements on a canvas — send email, wait X days, apply tag, notify user. It works well for straightforward follow-up sequences, invoice reminders, and lead nurturing. The problem is that anything beyond basic sequences gets complex quickly, and the UI hasn't aged as gracefully as the underlying logic.

GoHighLevel's automation (called "Workflows") is more modern and considerably more powerful. You can trigger automations from phone calls, form fills, appointment bookings, pipeline stage changes, SMS replies, Facebook Lead Ads, and more. You can run conditional branches, use AI-powered response bots, send voicemail drops, and even automate Google review requests — all inside the same workflow.

In my experience building automations for clients, GoHighLevel's Workflow builder takes longer to learn but pays off significantly once you understand it. I've built automations in GoHighLevel that would require Keap plus at least two additional tools (like a separate SMS platform and a review management app) to replicate.

For pure email automation simplicity, Keap has the edge in ease of use. For raw automation power and multi-channel reach, GoHighLevel wins.

## Which CRM Is Better for Agencies?

This one isn't close. GoHighLevel was built for agencies from day one. The core value proposition is simple: one account to manage all your clients, white-labeled under your brand, with the option to charge each client a monthly subscription.

Here's what makes GoHighLevel exceptional for agency use:

- **Sub-accounts**: Each client gets their own isolated account. One login, one dashboard, full separation of data.
- **Snapshot system**: Build a funnel, automation sequence, or entire business setup once, then clone it into any client account in seconds. This is a game-changer for agencies with clients in the same industry.
- **White-label SaaS mode**: On the Agency Pro plan at $497/mo, you can rebrand GoHighLevel as your own software, set your own pricing, and keep the margin. I've seen agencies charge clients $197–$497/mo for access to their branded platform. At scale, this alone makes the $497/mo plan a no-brainer.
- **Reporting and reputation management**: Centralized dashboards showing all client activity, plus automated review request campaigns that boost Google ratings.

Keap has no equivalent to any of these. It's a single-business tool. If you tried to manage ten clients on Keap, you'd need ten separate accounts at $299/mo each — $2,990/mo versus GoHighLevel's $297/mo for the same number of clients on the Unlimited plan.

## Where Does Keap Actually Win?

I want to be honest here because blindly recommending one tool over another doesn't help anyone make a good decision.

Keap genuinely wins in a few scenarios:

**1. E-commerce and payment automation.** Keap has a more mature native payment and invoicing system, with built-in order forms, checkout pages, and payment plan management that have been refined over 20+ years. If your business lives and dies by recurring billing and invoice follow-up, Keap's native experience is smoother.

**2. Simplicity for non-technical owners.** If a business owner wants to set up their own CRM without any outside help, Keap's onboarding is more guided and less overwhelming. GoHighLevel's power comes with complexity.

**3. Long-term data reliability.** Keap has a 20+ year track record. Their data infrastructure is proven. GoHighLevel is growing rapidly — which is exciting but also means the platform evolves quickly, and occasional bugs or UI changes come with the territory.

**4. Direct B2B sales teams.** Keap's pipeline and sales CRM features have subtle refinements that solo sales reps appreciate, particularly in how it handles tasks, follow-up reminders, and contact history.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q:** Is GoHighLevel better than Keap for a small business with under 500 contacts?
**A:** It depends on your goals. If you want simplicity and already know Keap, Keap works fine for a small contact list. But GoHighLevel's $97/mo Starter plan includes unlimited contacts, SMS, and a funnel builder — features you'd pay significantly more for in Keap. For price-to-value ratio, GoHighLevel wins even at small scale.

**Q:** Can GoHighLevel replace Keap entirely?
**A:** For most use cases, yes. GoHighLevel covers CRM, email, SMS, funnels, calendars, and more. The one area where Keap still has a slight edge is native e-commerce and payment plan automation, but GoHighLevel's payment features are improving rapidly.

**Q:** What happened to Infusionsoft? Is it the same as Keap?
**A:** Yes. Infusionsoft rebranded to Keap in 2019. The company behind both is the same — Keap is just a simplified, modernized version of the Infusionsoft product. Some legacy users still refer to it as Infusionsoft, but the platform is now marketed and sold as Keap.

**Q:** How long is the GoHighLevel free trial?
**A:** GoHighLevel offers a 14-day free trial. Keap also offers a 14-day trial. Neither platform offers a free plan, so you'll want to take advantage of the trial period to test the features most important to your business.

**Q:** Is GoHighLevel's $97/mo Starter plan enough for a growing agency?
**A:** The Starter plan works well for a single-location business or a solo operator. However, for agencies managing multiple clients, the Unlimited plan at $297/mo is more appropriate — it unlocks unlimited sub-accounts (client accounts) and removes per-account branding limitations. Most serious agency operators I know are on the Unlimited or Agency Pro plan.

**Q:** Does Keap have a white-label or reseller option?
**A:** No. Keap does not offer white-labeling or a reseller/partner program that lets you rebrand the platform as your own. GoHighLevel's Agency Pro plan at $497/mo is specifically designed for this use case.

**Q:** Which platform has better customer support?
**A:** Both have their challenges. Keap has phone support on higher plans, which some users prefer. GoHighLevel has a very active user community, a large Facebook group, and 24/7 chat support, but response quality can vary. In my experience, GoHighLevel's community support often resolves issues faster than waiting for official support channels.

## Conclusion

After spending real time implementing both platforms, my take is straightforward: if you run a digital marketing agency or plan to resell software to clients, GoHighLevel is the smarter investment — not even close. The economics alone justify it, and the feature set is genuinely deeper where it matters for agency workflows.

If you're a solo small business owner who needs a stable, simple CRM with good e-commerce follow-up and you're not interested in the agency angle, Keap is a legitimate choice — just be prepared to pay a significant premium for that simplicity.

For most people reading this, though, the combination of pricing, power, and growth potential makes GoHighLevel the clear winner. I'd recommend starting the 14-day free trial and spending time in the Workflow builder — that's where the platform's real value reveals itself.
