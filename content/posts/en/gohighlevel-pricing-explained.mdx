GoHighLevel pricing in 2026 is straightforward on the surface — three plans at $97, $297, and $497 per month — but the devil is in the details of what each tier actually unlocks. After implementing GoHighLevel for dozens of clients, I can tell you that choosing the wrong plan is the number-one mistake I see new users make.

## Key Takeaways

- GoHighLevel offers three plans: Starter ($97/mo), Unlimited ($297/mo), and Agency Pro/SaaS ($497/mo)
- The $97 Starter plan supports **1 sub-account (location)** — fine for a single business, limiting for agencies
- The $297 Unlimited plan removes the sub-account cap and adds a branded desktop app
- The $497 Agency Pro plan adds SaaS mode, letting you resell GoHighLevel as your own software
- All plans include a 14-day free trial, and usage-based costs (calls, SMS, email) are billed separately via Twilio/Mailgun

## What Are the Three GoHighLevel Plans?

Let me break down exactly what you get at each tier before we get into the "is it enough?" question.

### Starter — $97/month

The Starter plan is GoHighLevel's entry point and it's genuinely impressive for the price. You get:

- **1 sub-account (location)** — meaning the platform is configured for a single business
- Full CRM, pipeline management, and contact management
- Email marketing, SMS marketing, and voicemail drops
- Funnel and website builder (unlimited funnels and pages)
- Calendar and appointment booking
- Automation workflows
- Reputation management (Google/Facebook review requests)
- Basic reporting and analytics
- Chat widget and forms
- Two-way SMS and email conversations

The single sub-account limit is the real constraint here. If you ARE the business — a solo consultant, a coach, a local service provider — the Starter plan covers everything you need. I've run entire marketing operations for small businesses on this plan without hitting a wall.

### Unlimited — $297/month

The Unlimited plan is where agencies live. The jump from $97 to $297 unlocks:

- **Unlimited sub-accounts (locations)** — manage as many client accounts as you want under one roof
- Branded desktop app — you can white-label GoHighLevel with your own agency's branding
- API access for deeper custom integrations
- All features from the Starter plan

The math here is obvious for any agency managing even three or more clients. At $97 per client on separate Starter accounts you'd already be at $291/mo — and you'd lose the centralized management. The Unlimited plan at $297 lets you host unlimited clients for one flat fee.

### Agency Pro (SaaS Mode) — $497/month

Agency Pro is where the real profit potential lives. On top of everything in Unlimited, you get:

- **SaaS mode** — rebrand GoHighLevel completely and sell subscription access to your own clients
- Your own Stripe integration to charge clients directly through the platform
- Automated sub-account onboarding via snapshots
- Split testing for funnels
- Advanced AI tools and conversation AI features

SaaS mode deserves its own deep dive (and I cover it separately), but the short version: you can charge clients $197–$497/mo for "your" software, while paying $497/mo flat for unlimited seats. With just 3 clients at $197 each, you've already covered your platform cost and you're in profit.

## What Does GoHighLevel Cost Beyond the Monthly Fee?

This is where a lot of people get surprised. GoHighLevel itself is the subscription — but phone calls, SMS messages, and emails are billed separately at usage rates through Twilio and Mailgun (which GoHighLevel integrates natively).

Here's a rough breakdown of usage costs:

| Usage Type | Approximate Cost |
|---|---|
| Outbound SMS (US) | ~$0.0079 per segment |
| Inbound SMS (US) | ~$0.0075 per segment |
| Outbound calls (US) | ~$0.014 per minute |
| Inbound calls (US) | ~$0.0085 per minute |
| Emails (via Mailgun) | ~$0.001 per email |
| Phone number rental | ~$1.15/mo per number |

For a typical small business sending 500 SMS/month and making 200 minutes of calls, you're looking at roughly $10–$20 extra per month. That's not nothing, but it's far cheaper than most standalone VOIP or SMS platforms charge.

GoHighLevel also offers **LC Phone** (their own telephony system) which can simplify billing by rolling these costs into one GoHighLevel wallet top-up.

## Starter vs. Unlimited: The Real Decision

The question I get asked most often is whether the $97 Starter plan is "enough." Here's my honest answer:

**The Starter plan IS enough if:**
- You're a single-location business (restaurant, gym, clinic, consultant)
- You don't need to manage other businesses' accounts
- You're replacing tools like Calendly, Mailchimp, a basic CRM, and a landing page builder
- Budget is tight and you want to test the platform before scaling

**The Starter plan is NOT enough if:**
- You're a marketing agency managing client accounts
- You want to white-label the platform with your brand
- You need to onboard more than one client under your roof
- You plan to grow beyond a single business entity

For freelancers and solopreneurs, I've recommended the Starter plan to plenty of clients without hesitation. When I compare it against the [GoHighLevel vs HubSpot](/blog/gohighlevel-vs-hubspot) analysis I did, the $97 price point absolutely demolishes what HubSpot charges for similar functionality.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q:** Does GoHighLevel offer a free trial?
**A:** Yes, GoHighLevel offers a 14-day free trial on all plans. You can test the full platform before committing to a paid subscription.

**Q:** Can I switch between GoHighLevel plans?
**A:** Yes, you can upgrade or downgrade your plan at any time from your billing settings. Upgrades take effect immediately; downgrades apply at the next billing cycle.

**Q:** What happens to my sub-accounts if I downgrade from Unlimited to Starter?
**A:** If you downgrade to Starter and have more than one sub-account, you'll need to reduce your sub-accounts to one before the downgrade takes effect. GoHighLevel won't delete data automatically, but access will be restricted.

**Q:** Are there any hidden fees in GoHighLevel's pricing?
**A:** The monthly plan fee is transparent, but usage-based costs for SMS, calls, and emails are separate. These are billed through Twilio/Mailgun or GoHighLevel's LC Phone wallet. For most small businesses, this adds $10–$30/mo. Heavy SMS or call users will pay more.

**Q:** Is GoHighLevel's $97 plan enough for a marketing agency?
**A:** Almost certainly not. The Starter plan limits you to one sub-account, which means you can only manage one client's data. Agencies with multiple clients need the Unlimited plan at $297/mo.

**Q:** What is SaaS mode in GoHighLevel and which plan includes it?
**A:** SaaS mode is a feature that lets you white-label GoHighLevel and resell it to clients as your own software product, complete with your own branding and pricing. It's only available on the Agency Pro plan at $497/mo.

**Q:** How does GoHighLevel pricing compare to HubSpot?
**A:** GoHighLevel's $97 Starter plan includes features that would cost $400–$800/mo on HubSpot when you add CRM, Marketing Hub, and Service Hub together. For agencies and small businesses, GoHighLevel is significantly more cost-effective.

**Q:** Does GoHighLevel charge per contact or per email sent?
**A:** GoHighLevel does not charge per contact — you can have unlimited contacts on all plans. Emails sent via Mailgun or LC Email are charged at usage rates (around $0.001 per email), but the per-contact model used by tools like Mailchimp doesn't apply here.
