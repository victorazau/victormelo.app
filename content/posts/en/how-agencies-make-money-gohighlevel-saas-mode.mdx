## What Exactly Is GoHighLevel SaaS Mode?

SaaS mode is a feature inside the Agency Pro tier of GoHighLevel ($497/month) that transforms the platform into a white-labeled software product you can sell. Instead of just using GoHighLevel internally for your clients, you become the software vendor. Your clients log in to *your* branded platform — your logo, your domain, your colors — and they pay *you* a monthly fee for access.

Under the hood, it's still GoHighLevel doing the heavy lifting: the CRM, the funnel builder, the automation engine, the reputation management tools, two-way SMS and email, appointment scheduling, and everything else the platform offers. But from your client's perspective, they're using *YourBrand CRM* or *YourBrand Marketing Suite* — not GoHighLevel.

This is fundamentally different from simply reselling agency services. You're selling software access. That distinction matters enormously for your margins, your scalability, and how clients perceive the value they're getting.

## How the Money Actually Works

Let me walk through the math the way I actually think about it.

You pay $497/month for the Agency Pro plan. That gives you unlimited sub-accounts (client accounts) and access to SaaS mode. Your cost per client goes down with every account you add.

**Simple example — 20 clients at $297/month each:**

| Item | Monthly Amount |
|---|---|
| GoHighLevel Agency Pro plan | -$497 |
| 20 clients × $297/mo | +$5,940 |
| **Net monthly revenue** | **$5,443** |
| Annual run rate | ~$65,316 |

And that's before you layer in any done-for-you services, onboarding fees, or upsells. The platform cost becomes almost irrelevant once you have even 5-6 paying clients.

Most agencies I've talked to price their SaaS tiers somewhere between:
- **$97–$147/month** for a basic CRM/pipeline tier (small local businesses)
- **$197–$297/month** for a mid tier that includes automation and reputation management

You can also charge a one-time setup or onboarding fee ($250–$1,000 is common) which helps offset your time investment upfront.

## What Does SaaS Mode Actually Include?

When you activate SaaS mode, you get several specific capabilities beyond the standard Unlimited plan:

**White-label desktop app** — clients access the platform via a custom subdomain (e.g., crm.youragency.com) and see only your branding. The GoHighLevel name is hidden.

**Automated client billing via Stripe** — you connect your Stripe account, set up pricing plans inside GoHighLevel, and the platform handles recurring billing automatically. Clients enter their card once and get charged on your schedule.

**Snapshot deployment** — you build a "snapshot" (a templated account setup with pre-built funnels, automations, pipelines, and even sample data) and deploy it to new client accounts in minutes. This is the key to scalability. Instead of manually configuring each client from scratch, you clone your proven setup.

**Twilio and Mailgun rebilling** — you can mark up the cost of SMS, email, and phone usage and charge clients at a margin. Some agencies make an additional $20–$100/month per client just on usage rebilling.

**Custom mobile app (add-on)** — for an additional fee, you can have a fully branded iOS and Android app. This is a premium differentiator if you're selling to clients who want to look established.

## Who Should Actually Use SaaS Mode?

I want to be honest here because SaaS mode isn't for everyone — and getting this wrong wastes time and money.

**SaaS mode is a strong fit if you:**
- Already have 5+ clients you're doing marketing or CRM work for
- Serve a niche where you can build one great snapshot and replicate it (HVAC companies, real estate agents, med spas, gyms, law firms, etc.)
- Want predictable MRR instead of project-based income
- Have some appetite for a light software sales conversation with clients
- Can handle basic support questions or hire a VA to do it

**SaaS mode is probably NOT the right move if you:**
- Are brand new to the agency space with no existing clients
- Primarily do one-off projects with no recurring relationship
- Aren't willing to be the first line of support for the software
- Work in industries with very complex or bespoke CRM needs that GoHighLevel's templates don't handle well

I've seen agencies try to go straight to SaaS mode before they've validated their niche, and it typically doesn't end well. The snapshot only gets powerful once you've built it through real client work. Start with the Unlimited plan ($297/month), serve clients directly, build your snapshot, then upgrade to Agency Pro and flip the SaaS switch.

## How to Set Up SaaS Mode Step by Step (Overview)

I won't turn this into a click-by-click tutorial — that's better as video content — but here's the strategic sequence I follow when setting up SaaS mode for a new agency:

1. **Connect Stripe** to your GoHighLevel agency account (Settings → Payments)
2. **Build your snapshot** — set up one sub-account the way you'd want every client's account to look: pipelines, automations, email templates, funnel pages, reputation management settings, calendar setup. This is where you invest real time upfront.
3. **Configure your SaaS pricing plans** inside GoHighLevel's SaaS configurator — you'll define your tiers, feature access per tier, and Stripe pricing IDs
4. **Set up your white-label domain** — create a subdomain (e.g., app.youragency.com) and point it to GoHighLevel's servers
5. **Create your onboarding flow** — GoHighLevel has a built-in client onboarding workflow; customize it to match your brand
6. **Test it** by creating a test sub-account and going through the client experience yourself
7. **Start selling** — in my experience, the easiest first clients are existing retainer clients you convert from paying for services to paying for software access

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q:** Do I need the $497/month plan to use SaaS mode?
**A:** Yes. SaaS mode with automated client billing, white-labeling, and snapshot deployment is exclusive to the Agency Pro (SaaS) plan at $497/month. The Starter ($97/mo) and Unlimited ($297/mo) plans do not include SaaS mode capabilities.

**Q:** How many clients do I need to break even on the Agency Pro plan?
**A:** If you charge clients $97/month, you need 6 clients to cover the $497 platform cost. At $197/month per client, you only need 3. Most agencies I've worked with break even within 60–90 days of activating SaaS mode.

**Q:** Will my clients know they're using GoHighLevel?
**A:** Not unless you tell them. The white-label setup hides all GoHighLevel branding. Your clients see your logo, your domain, and your platform name. The mobile app (available as a paid add-on) also uses your branding on the App Store and Google Play.

**Q:** Can I still do done-for-you services alongside SaaS mode?
**A:** Absolutely, and most agencies do. SaaS mode handles the software layer. You can still charge separately for setup, ad management, content creation, or ongoing campaign management. Many agencies use SaaS as the base subscription and stack DFY services on top as upsells.

**Q:** What happens if a client cancels their subscription?
**A:** When a client's Stripe subscription lapses, you can configure their sub-account to be automatically paused or have their access restricted. You retain the data. This gives you a natural retention moment — you can reach out and reactivate before anything is permanently deleted.

**Q:** Is SaaS mode hard to set up technically?
**A:** The Stripe integration and domain configuration require some basic technical comfort, but GoHighLevel's onboarding documentation is solid. The hardest part isn't the technical setup — it's building a high-quality snapshot that actually delivers value. Budget a few days for that.

**Q:** Can I offer a free trial to my SaaS clients?
**A:** Yes. GoHighLevel's SaaS configurator supports trial periods so you can offer prospects a 14-day free trial before their first charge — mirroring the same model GoHighLevel uses to acquire its own customers.
