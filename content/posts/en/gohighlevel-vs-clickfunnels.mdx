GoHighLevel wins this comparison for most agencies and marketing consultants, while ClickFunnels holds its ground for solo entrepreneurs who live and die by funnel conversion rates. After implementing both platforms across dozens of client accounts, I can tell you the choice comes down to one fundamental question: are you running a client-services business, or are you a solopreneur selling your own products through optimized funnels?

## Key Takeaways

- GoHighLevel is an all-in-one agency platform with CRM, automation, SMS, email, websites, pipelines, and white-labeling — ClickFunnels is primarily a funnel and course builder.
- ClickFunnels starts at $97/mo but its most useful plan runs $297/mo; GoHighLevel's Starter plan is also $97/mo and replaces far more tools.
- If you manage clients, GoHighLevel's sub-account model and white-label capability give you a business model ClickFunnels simply cannot replicate.
- ClickFunnels 2.0 added a CRM, but in my experience it's still immature compared to GoHighLevel's battle-tested pipeline management.
- Neither platform is objectively "better" — they're built for different operators.

## What Are These Platforms, Exactly?

Before I get into the feature-by-feature breakdown, let me define what each tool actually is, because the marketing around both of them tends to overstate their scope.

**GoHighLevel** is an all-in-one sales and marketing platform built specifically for agencies and their clients. It consolidates a CRM, email marketing, SMS/MMS, pipeline management, funnel and website builder, booking calendars, reputation management, membership sites, and a white-label mobile app under one roof. The agency model is baked into the architecture — you get a master account and unlimited client sub-accounts (on the higher plans).

**ClickFunnels** is a funnel-building platform that has expanded over the years. The original ClickFunnels (1.0) was purely drag-and-drop funnel pages with one-click upsells and order bumps. ClickFunnels 2.0, launched in 2022, added a CRM, email marketing, blog, and courses. It's a real upgrade, but the DNA of the product is still "build a high-converting funnel and sell stuff through it."

I've used both extensively. GoHighLevel I run for my agency clients. ClickFunnels I've used to build product launches and course sales pages. They feel completely different in day-to-day use.

## Which Platform Has Better Pricing Value?

This is where GoHighLevel pulls significantly ahead for anyone running an agency or managing more than one brand.

GoHighLevel's pricing structure:
- **Starter** — $97/mo: 1 account, all core features, great for solo operators
- **Unlimited** — $297/mo: unlimited sub-accounts, unlimited contacts, API access, white-label desktop app
- **Agency Pro (SaaS mode)** — $497/mo: everything in Unlimited plus the ability to resell GoHighLevel as your own branded SaaS product

ClickFunnels 2.0 pricing (as of mid-2025):
- **Basic** — $97/mo: 1 workspace, 1 domain, limited funnels
- **Pro** — $297/mo: 3 workspaces, 3 domains, affiliate program access
- **Funnel Hacker** — $497/mo: unlimited workspaces, unlimited domains

Here's the honest math I walk my clients through: GoHighLevel's $297/mo Unlimited plan replaces tools like ActiveCampaign (~$79/mo), Calendly (~$16/mo), Birdeye or Podium for reviews (~$300/mo), Twilio for SMS (usage-based but often $50-100/mo for small agencies), and a basic website builder subscription. The stack replacement value is real — I've seen agencies cut their software bill by $400-600/mo after consolidating onto GoHighLevel.

ClickFunnels at $297/mo is a strong deal if funnels are your entire business model. But it doesn't replace your CRM, your SMS tool, your booking software, or your reputation management platform.

## Who Should Choose GoHighLevel?

In my experience, GoHighLevel is the right call when:

- **You run a marketing agency** serving local businesses, coaches, service providers, or e-commerce brands. The sub-account architecture is purpose-built for this.
- **You want to resell software**. The SaaS mode at $497/mo lets you white-label the entire platform and charge clients $97-$497/mo for their own branded version. I've seen agencies generate $5,000-$15,000/mo in pure SaaS revenue this way.
- **Your clients need SMS and calling**. Most local service businesses — HVAC, dental, real estate, legal — respond dramatically better to text than email. GoHighLevel's native SMS automation is one of its biggest competitive advantages.
- **You're consolidating a bloated tool stack**. If you're paying for separate tools for email, CRM, booking, forms, and review management, GoHighLevel can replace all of them.
- **You need pipeline and deal management**. GoHighLevel's visual pipeline is genuinely good — comparable to tools like Pipedrive — and it's included in the base plan.

## Who Should Stick With ClickFunnels?

I'll be honest: ClickFunnels still wins in specific scenarios, and I'd rather give you that truth than lose your trust.

- **You sell digital products with complex funnel flows**. One-click upsells, order bumps, and conversion-optimized checkout pages are still more polished in ClickFunnels. The page builder has more pre-built high-converting templates for e-commerce and info products.
- **You're a solopreneur focused on one offer**. If you have one course, one coaching program, or one digital product and your entire business is optimizing that funnel, ClickFunnels is a simpler, more focused tool.
- **You're in Russell Brunson's ecosystem**. The community, the books (DotCom Secrets, Expert Secrets), the events, and the affiliate program are massive. If that world resonates with you, the community value is real.
- **You don't manage clients**. GoHighLevel's multi-account architecture adds complexity you don't need if you're running a single brand.

## What About ClickFunnels 2.0 vs GoHighLevel's Funnel Builder?

This is a fair fight, and I want to give you a nuanced answer. ClickFunnels 2.0's funnel builder is still more intuitive for first-time users. The templates are beautiful, the split-testing workflow is clean, and the checkout/order bump functionality is ahead of GoHighLevel's.

GoHighLevel's funnel and website builder has improved dramatically over the past two years. For most agency use cases — landing pages, service pages, lead capture funnels — it's more than adequate. I build and deploy landing pages in GoHighLevel daily and they convert well. But if I were launching a $2,000 online course with a full ascension funnel, I'd still lean toward ClickFunnels for the funnel pages.

That said, GoHighLevel lets you run the entire customer journey — from ad click to booked call to automated follow-up to reputation review request — without leaving the platform. That operational continuity matters more than funnel polish for most of my clients.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q:** Can GoHighLevel replace ClickFunnels completely?
**A:** For most agency owners and service businesses, yes. GoHighLevel has a capable funnel and page builder, email automation, and checkout functionality. The only area where ClickFunnels still leads is conversion-optimized funnel templates and one-click upsell/order bump UX. If your business is primarily selling info products through elaborate funnels, you may want to keep ClickFunnels. For everything else, GoHighLevel handles it.

**Q:** Does GoHighLevel have a funnel builder?
**A:** Yes. GoHighLevel includes a drag-and-drop funnel and website builder with opt-in pages, sales pages, order forms, upsells, and thank-you pages. It supports custom domains, SSL, and integrates natively with GoHighLevel's CRM and automation workflows. It's not as polished as ClickFunnels, but it's functional and improves with every platform update.

**Q:** Is ClickFunnels 2.0 a real CRM?
**A:** ClickFunnels 2.0 added basic CRM functionality, including contact records and simple pipelines. However, it lacks the depth of GoHighLevel's CRM — no native SMS, no two-way calling, no reputation management, and the automation builder is less mature. For serious client relationship management, GoHighLevel's CRM is significantly more capable.

**Q:** What is GoHighLevel's free trial length?
**A:** GoHighLevel offers a 14-day free trial. ClickFunnels also offers a 14-day free trial. Neither platform currently offers a free tier.

**Q:** Which is better for a local service business — a dentist, realtor, or contractor?
**A:** GoHighLevel wins this decisively. Local service businesses benefit most from SMS follow-ups, appointment booking, missed-call text-back, and Google review automation — all of which are native GoHighLevel features. ClickFunnels has none of these. If I were setting up marketing infrastructure for a dental practice or roofing company, GoHighLevel would be my immediate recommendation.

**Q:** Can I use both GoHighLevel and ClickFunnels together?
**A:** Yes, and some marketers do. A common setup is building funnel pages in ClickFunnels for the conversion-optimized checkout experience, then pushing leads into GoHighLevel via webhook for CRM, SMS follow-up, and pipeline management. It adds complexity and cost, but if you're doing high-volume info product launches, it can be worth it.

**Q:** Which platform is easier to learn for beginners?
**A:** ClickFunnels has a simpler learning curve if your only goal is building a funnel. GoHighLevel has a steeper initial learning curve because there's simply more to learn — CRM, automations, SMS, pipelines, sub-accounts. Most of my clients are comfortable with GoHighLevel's core features within 2-3 weeks. The payoff in capability is worth the extra onboarding time.

## Conclusion

After running both platforms in real-world agency and client environments, my honest verdict is this: **GoHighLevel is the better platform for the majority of people reading this post** — agencies, consultants, and service businesses who need a complete operating system, not just a funnel builder.

ClickFunnels remains the stronger choice if you're a solopreneur selling digital products through complex, conversion-optimized funnels and you don't need CRM depth, SMS, or multi-client management.

The good news: both platforms offer a 14-day free trial, so you can test before you commit. I'd recommend starting with GoHighLevel if you're managing clients or building a recurring revenue business — the platform's depth will pay dividends as you scale. Start with the Starter plan at $97/mo and upgrade to Unlimited at $297/mo when you're ready to bring on clients or consolidate your tool stack.
